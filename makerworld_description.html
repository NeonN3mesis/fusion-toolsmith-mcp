Meshtastic / MeshCore RAK4631 Magnetic Solar Node Enclosure

Parametric weather-resistant solar enclosure for MeshCore / Meshtastic RAK4631 nodes, with magnetic mounting, N-type and SMA cover options, and an adjustable twist-lock painter's pole adapter.

A practical outdoor enclosure for solar-powered MeshCore or Meshtastic node and repeater builds. The stock setup is designed around a 60 x 110 mm solar panel, a RAKwireless WisBlock Mini / RAK4631-style radio, ring magnets, and an adjustable pole socket mount.

Use the standard cover for an N-type bulkhead pigtail, or use the SMA cover variant for builds with an SMA bulkhead connector.

Parametric Notes

This model exposes selected parameters in MakerWorld Parametric Model Maker. The stock configuration has been checked, but custom parameter changes can still create thin walls, broken features, or fit issues.

After generating a custom version, inspect all parts before printing.

Suggested parameter ranges:

  • WallThickness: 2.4-4.0 mm
  • PanelWidth: measure your actual solar panel body
  • PanelLength: measure your actual solar panel body
  • PanelThickness: 1.5-4.0 mm
  • BatteryWidth: measure your battery including wrap or connector bulges
  • BatteryThickness: measure your battery including wrap or connector bulges
  • PoleSocketDiameter: adjust to fit your pole; default is 17.5 mm
  • PoleAngle: 0-45 deg
  • PoleRotation: 0-360 deg
  • VentSlotCount: whole numbers only, suggested 4-12

Large changes may require checking clearances around the battery cavity, solar panel pocket, magnet pockets, and pole adapter.

Recommended Print Settings

  • Filament: ASA or PETG recommended for outdoor use
  • Avoid: PLA for outdoor installs, especially in direct sun
  • Walls: 3-4 walls
  • Infill: 15-20% gyroid
  • Supports: use slicer judgment for your printer, material, and selected orientation
  • Backplate orientation: print resting on the 45 degree chamfer along the bottom edge of the backplate
  • Cover orientation: print inverted

ASA is preferred for UV and heat resistance. PETG is a good easier-printing option for many outdoor uses.

Assembly / Fit Notes

  • Test-fit the solar panel, battery, magnets, and antenna connector before final assembly.
  • If sliding or mating parts are too tight, increase the relevant clearance or lightly clean up the printed surfaces.
  • For pole mounting, adjust PoleAngle and PoleRotation before generating the final print.
  • For custom pole sockets, adjust PoleSocketDiameter and inspect the generated internal thread before printing.
